About

What is therapy?

Therapy, also known as counseling or psychotherapy, is a process where a trained therapist helps you address and resolve personal issues, mental health conditions, and emotional difficulties through discussion and various techniques.

How do I know if I need therapy?

You might benefit from therapy if you're experiencing persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or anger, struggling to cope with daily life, facing relationship difficulties, or dealing with trauma. Therapy can help with a wide range of issues, not just severe mental health conditions.

What can I expect in a therapy session?

In a therapy session, you can expect to talk about your thoughts, feelings, and experiences with your therapist. The therapist will listen, provide support, and help you gain insights into your issues. They may also teach you coping skills and strategies to manage your problems effectively.

How long does therapy take?

The duration of therapy varies depending on individual needs and goals. Some people may feel better after a few sessions, while others might need ongoing support for several months or even years. Your therapist will work with you to develop a treatment plan that suits your situation.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes, therapy is generally confidential. Therapists are bound by ethical and legal guidelines to keep what you share private, with a few exceptions. These exceptions include situations where there is a risk of harm to yourself or others, or if there is a legal obligation to disclose certain information.

Counselling

Life demands flexibility, and my approach to therapy mirrors this necessity. I offer both in-person and online sessions to accommodate your busy schedule and seamlessly integrate therapy into your life. Whether you need long-term or short-term therapy, I tailor my services to your unique needs and goals. Individual Therapy

Individual therapy focuses on you and your personal growth. I work one-on-one with you to address your mental health needs, including stress, anxiety, depression, and personal issues. Sessions are tailored to help you understand yourself better, develop coping strategies, and work towards a healthier mindset. My goal is to support you in finding balance and achieving your personal goals.

Couples Therapy

Couples therapy helps partners improve their relationship by addressing communication issues, conflicts, and other challenges. I provide a safe space for both partners to express their feelings, understand each other better, and work towards mutual goals. Whether you're facing specific problems or just want to strengthen your relationship, couples therapy can provide valuable insights and tools.

Family Therapy

Family therapy addresses issues affecting the health and functioning of a family unit. It aims to foster better communication, resolve conflicts, and enhance relationships among family members. Whether dealing with a specific crisis or ongoing issues, family therapy helps each member understand their role and how they can contribute to a healthier, more supportive family dynamic.

Group Therapy

Group therapy involves one or more therapists working with a small group of clients together. It's often used for addressing issues like anxiety, depression, and addiction, allowing participants to share experiences and support each other. Group therapy provides a sense of community and helps individuals realize they are not alone in their struggles.

Child and Adolescent Therapy

Child and adolescent therapy is tailored to the developmental needs of younger clients. It addresses issues such as behavioral problems, anxiety, depression, and trauma, using techniques suited for children and teens. This type of therapy provides a safe and supportive environment for young clients to express themselves and work through their challenges.
